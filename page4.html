David Foster Wallace

For my senior Independent Study project, I plan to study the author David Foster Wallace. Wallace is a postmodern author, best known for his 1996 novel Infinite Jest. He also published two other novels, multiple short fiction collections, and journalistic articles. As a double major in English and Religious Studies, I believe that Wallace’s body of work is a unique example of the role religion plays in contemporary American literature. For my English junior IS, I wrote about the themes of addiction, suffering, and spirituality within Infinite Jest. I am currently continuing to study Infinite Jest for my Religious Studies junior IS, with a specific focus on ideas of religion and spirituality within the novel. For my senior IS, I would like to explore all of these themes in more detail, within Infinite Jest and Wallace’s larger body of work, including The Pale King, and his short fiction. I recieved a Copeland Funding Grant from the College of Wooster to travel to the University of Texas at Austin, where Wallace's archives are located, and I will conduct preliminary research for my senior IS there this summer. There, I will be able to explore early annotated drafts of Wallace’s work, as well as Wallace’s correspondences, and his personal essays and unpublished writings.
